The Vinland Sagas tell the story of Norse explorers, led by Leif Erikson, who reached North America around 1000 AD, long before Columbus. According to the sagas, Leif discovered a land called Vinland, rich with wild grapes and resources. His brother, Thorvald, later explored the area but was killed in a conflict with the native inhabitants, referred to as Skraelings. Other Norse expeditions, like that of Thorfinn Karlsefni, attempted to settle Vinland but eventually abandoned it due to continued conflicts and hardships. These sagas highlight the adventurous nature of the Vikings, and archaeological evidence supports their presence in places like L'Anse aux Meadows in Newfoundland.

Berserkers were fierce Viking warriors, known for their ferocity in battle, often fighting in a trance-like fury. The term "berserk" comes from the Old Norse word "berserkr," which possibly means "bear shirt," referring to warriors who wore animal pelts, particularly bear or wolf skins, to embody the strength of these beasts. Berserkers were said to enter a wild, uncontrollable rage, becoming immune to pain and attacking enemies with unmatched savagery. Their frenzied state made them both feared and revered in Norse society, and they often fought on the front lines of battles, charging recklessly at their foes, seemingly possessed by a supernatural force. The legend of the berserkers has been immortalized in Norse sagas and mythology.

Hajime no Ippo is a Japanese boxing manga and anime series created by George Morikawa. It follows the journey of Ippo Makunouchi, a shy high school student who is constantly bullied. His life changes when he's saved by professional boxer Mamoru Takamura, who introduces Ippo to the world of boxing. Under the guidance of coach Genji Kamogawa, Ippo discovers his natural strength and determination, starting his path to becoming a professional boxer. The series explores Ippo's growth as a boxer, his intense training, and his rivalries in the ring, particularly against fighters like Ichiro Miyata and Takeshi Sendou. Known for its detailed portrayal of boxing techniques and the emotional depth of its characters, Hajime no Ippo blends thrilling action with heartfelt moments of friendship, perseverance, and self-discovery.
